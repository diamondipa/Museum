Our dedicated professionals uncover, preserve, and interpret humanity's shared heritage through scientific research and cultural interpretation.

Dr. David Miller

Senior Field Archaeologist | Mesoamerican Specialist

1. Subject Matter Expertise

Archaeologist specializing in ceramic analysis and settlement patterns of pre-Columbian Mesoamerica, with a focus on the Maya civilization. 20+ years of field experience.

2. Professional Bio

Dr. Miller earned his PhD from Stanford University, studying under Dr. Elena Martinez. His dissertation on Teotihuacan trade networks won the Society for American Archaeology's Dissertation Award. With expertise in stratigraphic analysis and ceramic typology, he employs both traditional excavation techniques and modern technology including ground-penetrating radar and photogrammetry. David is known for his meticulous attention to detail and patient, systematic approach to excavation.

3. Museum Role

Senior Research Archaeologist and Field Director. Leads the museum's ongoing excavation at the recently discovered "White Snake Complex" in the Yucatán. Curates the Mesoamerican Pottery Collection and mentors junior field archaeologists.

4. Special Considerations

Recipient of the National Geographic Explorer Grant (2023). Fluent in Spanish and conversational Yucatec Maya. Developed an innovative ceramic dating method combining thermoluminescence with stylistic analysis. His mixed-heritage background informs his bicultural approach to interpretation.

5. Tools & Technology

Total station surveying equipment, drone photogrammetry, portable XRF analyzer, field conservation kit, and custom database software for artifact cataloging.

6. Personal Philosophy

"Every pottery shard holds a story of human connection. We're not just studying objects—we're reconstructing conversations across centuries."

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Physical Anthropologist | Bioarchaeology Specialist

1. Subject Matter Expertise

Physical Anthropologist specializing in paleopathology and isotopic analysis of human remains to reconstruct ancient health, diet, and migration patterns. PhD in Anthropology from University of Oxford.

2. Professional Bio

Dr. Johnson completed her doctorate at Oxford University with research on Neolithic population movements. She conducted postdoctoral research at the Max Planck Institute, developing novel methods for stable isotope analysis from dental calculus. With expertise in osteology, she can identify markers of disease, trauma, and nutritional stress in skeletal remains. Sarah has published over 30 peer-reviewed articles on ancient DNA preservation in various climatic conditions.

3. Museum Role

Director of Bioarchaeological Research. Oversees the ethical curation and study of human remains in the museum's collection. Leads collaborative projects with geneticists and climate scientists to understand human adaptation through time.

4. Special Considerations

Holds the distinguished Wenner-Gren Fellowship for Anthropological Research. Developed museum protocols for ethical engagement with descendant communities. Her innovative work on ancient disease patterns has revolutionized our understanding of prehistoric health challenges.

5. Tools & Technology

Scanning electron microscope, mass spectrometer for isotopic analysis, 3D bone scanning equipment, histological thin-section preparation tools, and specialized software for population genetics analysis.

6. Personal Philosophy

"Bones remember what history forgets. Through skeletal analysis, we give voice to those who can no longer speak for themselves."

Dr. Michael Chen

Cultural Interpreter | Narrative Ethnographer

1. Interpretive Discipline

Cultural Semiotician & Narrative Ethnographer - Specializes in decoding symbolic systems and creating multivocal narratives from material culture. Former director of the Metropolitan Art Museum.

2. Professional Bio

Dr. Chen earned dual PhDs in Cultural Anthropology and Comparative Literature from University of Chicago. His interdisciplinary approach blends post-colonial theory with indigenous storytelling traditions. Before joining the museum, he worked with First Nations communities to co-create heritage interpretation that centers indigenous knowledge systems. Michael has developed innovative exhibition frameworks that present multiple perspectives simultaneously, challenging singular historical narratives.

3. Museum Role

Chief Interpretation Officer. Leads teams that develop exhibition narratives, educational programs, and digital content. Facilitates community partnerships to ensure diverse voices shape how collections are presented and understood.

4. Special Considerations

Recipient of the American Alliance of Museums' Excellence in Interpretation Award (2024). Fluent in Mandarin, French, and Spanish. Currently leading the museum's decolonization initiative, which has facilitated the repatriation of sacred objects to indigenous communities.

5. Interpretive Method

Employs "story circle" methodology, bringing together archaeologists, descendant community members, artists, and historians to co-create interpretations. Uses digital storytelling platforms to present alternative narratives alongside traditional labels. Develops sensory-rich exhibition environments that engage visitors emotionally and intellectually.

6. Personal Philosophy

"Objects are questions, not answers. Our role is to create spaces where visitors can explore the questions with us, bringing their own experiences and perspectives to the conversation."
